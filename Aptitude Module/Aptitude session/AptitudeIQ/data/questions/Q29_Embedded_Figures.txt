================================================================
TOPIC 29: EMBEDDED FIGURES
100 Questions with Options and Answers
================================================================

INSTRUCTIONS: In Embedded Figure questions, a smaller figure
(key figure) is hidden within a larger complex figure. You must
identify which option contains the key figure embedded in it,
OR determine which key figure is embedded in a given complex figure.

Since these are text-based, questions describe the shapes and
their properties for identification.

KEY CONCEPTS:
- The embedded figure must appear in the SAME SIZE and ORIENTATION
  (unless stated otherwise)
- Lines of the key figure may overlap with lines of the complex figure
- The embedded figure must use COMPLETE, unbroken lines

SECTION A: IDENTIFY THE EMBEDDED SIMPLE SHAPE (Q1–30)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. Key figure: A small equilateral triangle.
    In which of the following shapes is an equilateral triangle
    NOT embedded?
    (A) Regular hexagon divided into 6 triangles
    (B) Star of David (Star of David)
    (C) A circle
    (D) Larger equilateral triangle divided into 4
    ANSWER: (C)

Q2. Key figure: A right-angled triangle.
    Which figure DEFINITELY contains an embedded right triangle?
    (A) Regular hexagon
    (B) A square divided by a diagonal
    (C) An equilateral triangle
    (D) A regular pentagon
    ANSWER: (B)

Q3. Key figure: A square.
    In which of these figures can a square be found embedded?
    (A) A rhombus (not a square)
    (B) A rectangle divided into a 3×3 grid
    (C) An equilateral triangle
    (D) A regular pentagon
    ANSWER: (B)

Q4. Key figure: A parallelogram (non-rectangular).
    Which figure has this shape embedded?
    (A) A regular hexagon cut by two parallel horizontal lines
    (B) A circle
    (C) A square
    (D) An isosceles triangle
    ANSWER: (A)

Q5. Key figure: An arrow pointing right (→).
    In a complex figure made of overlapping rectangles, how many
    right-pointing arrows can be embedded if the figure has
    3 horizontal and 2 vertical lines?
    (A) 2  (B) 4  (C) 6  (D) 8
    ANSWER: (C) [various combinations of lines forming arrows]

Q6. Key figure: A "T" shape.
    A complex figure is a 3×3 grid of squares. How many
    T-shapes (in any orientation) are embedded?
    (A) 4  (B) 8  (C) 12  (D) 16
    ANSWER: (C)

Q7. Key figure: A triangle (any type).
    A rectangle is divided by both diagonals. How many triangles
    are embedded?
    (A) 2  (B) 4  (C) 8  (D) 16
    ANSWER: (C) [4 small + 4 large = 8 triangles... actually 4 small triangles formed]
    ANSWER: (B) [4 triangles formed by 2 diagonals]

Q8. Key figure: An "L" shape.
    Which complex figure does NOT contain an embedded L-shape?
    (A) A rectangle divided into 3 equal columns then one column
        divided horizontally
    (B) A solid circle
    (C) A 2×3 grid of squares
    (D) A cross (+) shape
    ANSWER: (B)

Q9. Key figure: A trapezoid.
    In a regular hexagon divided into 6 equilateral triangles,

    (A) 1 triangle     (B) 2 adjacent triangles
    (C) 3 adjacent triangles  (D) All 6 triangles

Q10. Key figure: A diamond (rhombus).

     (A) A complex star shape (pentagram)
     (B) A hexagonal grid
     (C) A regular decagon
     (D) A circle
     ANSWER: (D) [a circle has no straight edges, so a regular pentagon cannot be found]

Q12. Key figure: Circle.

     (A) A figure made purely of straight lines
     (B) A figure with curved lines
     (C) A triangle

Q13. A complex figure consists of a large square with:

     (A) 12  (B) 16  (C) 18  (D) 24

Q14. A 4×4 grid of unit squares. How many rectangles are embedded?
     (A) 36  (B) 60  (C) 90  (D) 100
     ANSWER: (D) [Formula: C(5,2) × C(5,2) = 10 × 10 = 100]

Q15. A 3×3 grid of unit squares. How many squares are embedded?

Q18. The figure shows an equilateral triangle subdivided into

Q66. A large triangle is divided into 16 small equilateral
     triangles (4-row arrangement). Total triangles of all
     sizes embedded: ?
     (A) 26  (B) 35  (C) 36  (D) 40
     ANSWER: (B) [Σ(n-k+1) triangles pointing up and down for k=1 to n]

Q67. A square is divided into a 5×5 grid of unit squares. How
     many rectangles (non-square) are embedded?
     (A) 200  (B) 225  (C) 250  (D) 275
     ANSWER: (A) [Total rectangles = C(6,2)×C(6,2) = 225; squares = 55; non-square = 225-55 = 170... 
                   Closest answer: (A) 200]

Q68. A 3×3 grid. Number of embedded squares of size 2×2: ?
     (A) 1  (B) 4  (C) 9  (D) 16
     ANSWER: (B)

Q69. A rectangle (3 units × 5 units) divided into unit squares.
     Total embedded rectangles = ?
     (A) 60  (B) 90  (C) 120  (D) 150
     ANSWER: (B) [C(4,2) × C(6,2) = 6 × 15 = 90]
