STANDARD DICE RULES (for reference):
- Opposite faces sum to 7: (1↔6), (2↔5), (3↔4)
- When 1 faces you and 2 is on top → 3 is to your right
  (standard Western die orientation)

SECTION A: STANDARD DICE — OPPOSITE FACES & ORIENTATION (Q1–30)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. On a standard die, if the face showing 3 is facing you, and
    4 is on top, which number is at the bottom?
    (A) 2  (B) 3  (C) 1  (D) 6
    ANSWER: (B) [opposite of 4 is 3... wait: opposite of top(4) = bottom(3)]
    ANSWER: (B) [3 is at bottom since 4+3=7]

Q2. On a standard die, the face opposite to 5 is: ?
    (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 3  (D) 6
    ANSWER: (B)

Q3. On a standard die, the face opposite to 1 is: ?
    (A) 2  (B) 4  (C) 6  (D) 5
    ANSWER: (C)

Q4. Three faces of a die have values 1, 2, and 3 meeting at
    one corner. What is the sum of the three faces NOT visible
    (the other 3 faces)?
    (A) 12  (B) 15  (C) 18  (D) 21
    ANSWER: (C) [Total = 21; visible = 6; others = 15]
    ANSWER: (B) [visible sum=1+2+3=6; total=21; hidden=15]

Q5. A die is placed on a table. Top face = 6, front face = 2.
    What is the right-face number on a standard die?
    (A) 1  (B) 3  (C) 4  (D) 5
    ANSWER: (B)

Q6. Two dice are placed side by side. The faces touching each
    other sum to 7. The left die shows 3 on top. The touching
    face of the left die = 4. What does the right die show on
    the face touching the left die?
    (A) 3  (B) 4  (C) 5  (D) 6
    ANSWER: (A) [7-4=3]

Q7. A standard die is rolled. It shows 4 on top. What is the
    sum of all visible faces (top + 4 sides visible)?
    (A) 14  (B) 17  (C) 18  (D) 21
    ANSWER: (C) [Total all 6 = 21; bottom(3) hidden; visible = 21-3 = 18]

Q8. On a die, if 1 is on the top and 2 faces you, which
    number is on the right?
    (A) 3  (B) 4  (C) 5  (D) 6
    ANSWER: (A)

Q9. A die has: top=5, front=3, right=2. What is the bottom? ?
    (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 4  (D) 6
    ANSWER: (B) [opposite of 5 is 2]

Q10. A die shows: top=2, right=4. The front face is: ?
     (A) 1  (B) 3  (C) 5  (D) 6
     ANSWER: (B) [standard orientation: top=2, right=4, front=3]

Q11. Two standard dice are stacked. The touching faces sum to 7.
     Top die shows 5 on top. Bottom of top die = 2. Top of
     bottom die = 5. Bottom die shows what on top (touching
     the upper die)?
     (A) 2  (B) 3  (C) 5  (D) 7
     ANSWER: (C) [since touching faces sum to 7: if bottom of top die=2, top of bottom die=5]

Q12. Three standard dice are stacked. Sum of all hidden faces
     (touching faces between dice + bottom face of stack): ?
     (A) 21  (B) 28  (C) 35  (D) 42
     ANSWER: (B) [Each pair of touching faces = 7; plus bottom face.
                   2 pairs × 7 = 14; bottom of lowest die (unknown, but
                   total = 3 × 21 = 63; visible top + 4 sides = complex]
     [Standard answer: hidden faces between = 7 each pair = 14; bottom = unknown]
     [If top of stack shows X, bottom of stack = 7-X; total hidden = 7+7+bottom of stack]
     ANSWER: (B) [Generally: 2 pairs of touching faces (14) + bottom = 14+7 = 21 minimum]
     [Correct standard approach: 28 if considering all hidden faces in 3-dice stack]

Q13. On a non-standard die, opposite faces are: (1,2), (3,5),
     (4,6). If top=3, front=1, what is the right face?
     (A) 4  (B) 5  (C) 6  (D) 2
     ANSWER: (C) [with these opposites, right face = 6 based on orientation]

Q14. A die is rolled: it shows 3. It is rolled again landing on
     an adjacent face. Which of the following CANNOT be the
     result?
     (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 4  (D) 6
     ANSWER: (D) [6 is opposite to 1... wait: 3's opposite is 4. 
                   Adjacent to 3 = 1, 2, 5, 6. So 4 is NOT adjacent]
     ANSWER: (C) [4 is opposite to 3, so cannot be adjacent]

Q15. A die is placed: 1 on top, 2 facing you. The die is tilted
     toward you (top falls toward you). New top face = ?
     (A) 2  (B) 3  (C) 5  (D) 6
     ANSWER: (A)

Q16. A die: 6 on top, 5 facing you. Die is tilted RIGHT. New top = ?
     (A) 3  (B) 4  (C) 2  (D) 1
     ANSWER: (B) [right face comes to top]

Q17. Two identical dice show the same face up (4). The probability
     that both bottom faces are also the same is: ?
     (A) 0  (B) 1/6  (C) 1  (D) 1/36
     ANSWER: (C) [if top=4 on both, bottom=3 on both → always same]

Q18. A die is rolled. Top = 2. Die rotated 90° clockwise (viewed
     from above). Top remains: ?
     (A) 2  (B) 3  (C) 4  (D) 5
     ANSWER: (A) [rotating around vertical axis doesn't change top]

Q19. A standard die: if you look from above and see 6, and the
     die is rotated 180° around the vertical axis, the top is: ?
     (A) 1  (B) 5  (C) 6  (D) 3

Q20. In a standard die, if 3 is facing you, which number is
     directly behind the die (the back face)?
     (A) 4  (B) 3  (C) 6  (D) 5

Q21. Two dice are thrown. What is the probability that both

     (A) 1/6  (B) 1/12  (C) 1/36  (D) 5/36
     ANSWER: (A)

Q22. A die is rolled 4 times. It shows 1, 2, 3, 4 in order.
     The face that appeared LEAST so far is: ?
     (A) 5  (B) 6  (C) 5 and 6  (D) All equal

Q23. Die: top=1, front=3. Die is rolled FORWARD once (top moves
     to front). New top = ?
     (A) 3  (B) 4  (C) 5  (D) 6
     ANSWER: (B) [new top = old back = opposite of front(3) = 4]

Q24. Die: top=1, front=3. Die rolled forward, then right.
     New top = ?
     (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 3  (D) 4
     ANSWER: (B)

Q25. Which two faces are adjacent (NOT opposite) on a standard die?
     (A) 1 and 6  (B) 3 and 4  (C) 2 and 5  (D) 1 and 3

Q26. Four dice are stacked. Sum of all hidden touching faces = ?
     (Assuming touching faces always sum to 7)
     (A) 21  (B) 28  (C) 35  (D) 42
     ANSWER: (A) [3 pairs of touching faces × 7 = 21]

Q27. A die has the number 6 replaced with a blank. The probability
     of rolling a blank is: ?
     (A) 0  (B) 1/6  (C) 1/3  (D) 1/2
     ANSWER: (B)

Q28. On a die, sum of all faces = ?
     (A) 15  (B) 18  (C) 21  (D) 24
     ANSWER: (C)

Q29. A die: top=4, front=5. If die is rolled LEFT once, new front = ?
     (A) 3  (B) 4  (C) 5  (D) 6
     ANSWER: (B) [rolling left: right face comes to front]

Q30. A standard die with 1 on top and 3 facing you. Which number
     is on the left face?
     (A) 2  (B) 4  (C) 5  (D) 6
     ANSWER: (A)

SECTION B: PAINTED/CUT CUBE PROBLEMS (Q31–60)

Q31. A large cube is painted red on all faces, then cut into
     27 equal small cubes. How many small cubes have exactly
     3 red faces?
     (A) 4  (B) 6  (C) 8  (D) 12
     ANSWER: (C) [8 corner cubes]

Q32. From Q31: how many small cubes have exactly 2 red faces?
     (A) 8  (B) 10  (C) 12  (D) 16
     ANSWER: (C) [12 edge cubes]

Q33. From Q31: how many small cubes have exactly 1 red face?
     (A) 4  (B) 6  (C) 8  (D) 10
     ANSWER: (B) [6 face-center cubes]

Q34. From Q31: how many small cubes have NO red faces?
     (A) 0  (B) 1  (C) 2  (D) 3
     ANSWER: (B) [1 center cube]

Q35. A cube is painted on all 6 faces and cut into 64 equal
     smaller cubes (4×4×4). How many small cubes have 3 painted
     faces?
     (A) 4  (B) 6  (C) 8  (D) 12
     ANSWER: (C) [always 8 corner cubes, regardless of cuts]

Q36. From Q35: cubes with exactly 2 painted faces?
     (A) 12  (B) 24  (C) 36  (D) 48
     ANSWER: (B) [each edge has 4-2=2 inner edge cubes; 12 edges × 2 = 24]

Q37. From Q35: cubes with exactly 1 painted face?
     (A) 6  (B) 12  (C) 18  (D) 24
     ANSWER: (D) [each face has (4-2)²=4 center cubes; 6 faces × 4 = 24]

Q38. From Q35: cubes with no painted faces?
     (A) 4  (B) 6  (C) 8  (D) 10
     ANSWER: (C) [(4-2)³ = 2³ = 8]

Q39. A cube (3×3×3) is painted only on the top and bottom faces.
     Small cubes with exactly 1 painted face: ?
     (A) 6  (B) 9  (C) 10  (D) 18
     ANSWER: (D) [top face: 9; bottom face: 9 = 18 (no overlap)]

Q40. A cube (3×3×3) is painted on 4 faces (all except top and
     bottom). Small cubes with 2 painted faces = ?
     (A) 4  (B) 8  (C) 12  (D) 16
     ANSWER: (B) [4 vertical edges × 2 cubes per edge = 8, but only if 4 faces painted = only vertical edges get 2 colors]

Q41. A 4×4×4 cube is cut into 64 small cubes. Total small cubes
     at corners (3-face) + edges (2-face) + faces (1-face) + inside
     (0-face) = ?
     (A) 56  (B) 60  (C) 64  (D) 68
     ANSWER: (C)

Q42. A cube is cut into 125 smaller cubes (5×5×5). Small cubes
     with no painted faces = ?
     (A) 9  (B) 16  (C) 25  (D) 27
     ANSWER: (D) [(5-2)³ = 3³ = 27]

Q43. A cube (n×n×n) when cut into small cubes, the number of
     cubes with 3 painted faces is always: ?
     (A) n  (B) 4  (C) 8  (D) 12
     ANSWER: (C)

Q44. A cube is painted RED on top, BLUE on bottom, GREEN on
     remaining 4 faces. Cut into 27. Cubes with 1 red and 1
     green face: ?
     (A) 2  (B) 4  (C) 8  (D) 12
     ANSWER: (B) [top 4 edges = 4 cubes with 1 red + 1 green]

Q45. A 3×3×3 cube is painted and cut. Cubes with exactly 2
     colors on them = cubes with 2 painted faces = ?
     (A) 8  (B) 10  (C) 12  (D) 16
     ANSWER: (C)

Q46. A cube is cut into 8 equal smaller cubes (2×2×2). All
     smaller cubes have: ?
     (A) 3 painted faces each (if original painted all 6)
     (B) 2 painted faces each
     (C) 1 painted face each
     (D) 0 painted faces each
     ANSWER: (A) [all 8 are corner cubes]

Q47. A solid wooden cube is painted red. Then ONE face is sanded
     off (paint removed from that face). Cut into 27 small cubes.
     How many have red on exactly 3 faces?
     (A) 4  (B) 6  (C) 8  (D) 10
     ANSWER: (A) [4 corners of the sanded face now have only 2 red faces; remaining 4 corners have 3 red faces]

Q48. A 3×3×3 cube: only the top face is painted. How many small
     cubes have paint?
     (A) 3  (B) 6  (C) 9  (D) 12
     ANSWER: (C) [top layer = 9 cubes, all painted on top]

Q49. From Q48: how many have EXACTLY 1 painted face?
     (A) 1  (B) 4  (C) 5  (D) 9
     ANSWER: (D) [all 9 in top layer get exactly 1 painted face (top)]

Q50. A 4×4×4 cube: painted on all 6 faces. Total small cubes = 64.
     Cubes with 0 painted faces = 8. Cubes with 1 painted face = ?
     (A) 18  (B) 24  (C) 28  (D) 32
     ANSWER: (B)

Q51. A large cube (6×6×6) is painted and cut into 216 unit cubes.
     Cubes with no paint = ?
     (A) 16  (B) 32  (C) 48  (D) 64
     ANSWER: (D) [(6-2)³ = 4³ = 64]

Q52. A cube is painted on alternate faces only (3 faces, no two
     adjacent). Cut into 27. Cubes with 3 painted faces = ?
     (A) 0  (B) 1  (C) 2  (D) 8

Q53. Two 3×3×3 cubes are painted (all 6 faces) and cut into

Q54. A 3×3×3 cube: painted on top, bottom, and one side face.
     Small cubes with exactly 2 painted faces = ?
     (A) 2  (B) 3  (C) 4  (D) 6

Q55. A cube: painted on all 6 faces; the paint penetrates 1 layer

     (A) Sphere  (B) Cube  (C) Tetrahedron  (D) Cylinder

Q56. A wooden cube is cut into 3 equal layers (3 slabs). Each slab

     (A) 9  (B) 18  (C) 27  (D) 36

Q57. A 2×3×4 rectangular block is cut into 1×1×1 unit cubes.
     Total small cubes = ?
     (A) 9  (B) 18  (C) 24  (D) 36

Q58. The same 2×3×4 block (all faces painted) is cut into unit
     cubes. Cubes with 3 painted faces = ?
     (A) 4  (B) 6  (C) 8  (D) 12
     ANSWER: (C) [8 corner cubes always]

Q59. From Q58: cubes with exactly 0 painted faces = ?
     (A) 0  (B) 2  (C) 4  (D) 6
     ANSWER: (B) [(2-2)(3-2)(4-2) = 0×1×2 = 0]
     ANSWER: (A) [no interior cubes in a 2-unit-thick block]

Q60. A 5×5×5 cube painted all faces. Cubes with exactly 1 painted
     face = ?
     (A) 24  (B) 36  (C) 48  (D) 54
     ANSWER: (D) [each face: (5-2)² = 9; 6 faces × 9 = 54]

     ANSWER: (D) [top = 3 after 3 forward rolls... let me recalculate]
