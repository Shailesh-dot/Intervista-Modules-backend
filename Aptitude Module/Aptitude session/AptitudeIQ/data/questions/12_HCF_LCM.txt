Q1. Find the HCF of 36 and 48.
    (A) 6  (B) 12  (C) 18  (D) 24
    ANSWER: B
    EXPLANATION: 36=2²×3², 48=2⁴×3. HCF=2²×3=12.

Q2. Find the LCM of 8 and 12.
    (A) 48  (B) 24  (C) 96  (D) 36
    ANSWER: B
    EXPLANATION: 8=2³, 12=2²×3. LCM=2³×3=24.

Q3. The product of two numbers is 4800. Their HCF is 20. Find the LCM.
    (A) 240  (B) 200  (C) 480  (D) 120
    ANSWER: A
    EXPLANATION: LCM=Product/HCF=4800/20=240.

Q4. Find the HCF of 12, 18, and 24.
    (A) 3  (B) 6  (C) 12  (D) 2

     EXPLANATION: 12a+12b=84 → a+b=7. Co-prime pairs: (1,6),(2,5),(3,4). But (3,4) gives HCF(36,48)=12 ✓. (1,6)→HCF(12,72)=12✓. (2,5)→HCF(24,60)=12✓. That's 3 pairs. ANSWER: B.

Q86. Find smallest number that leaves remainder 2 when divided by 3, 5, or 7.
     (A) 107  (B) 212  (C) 2  (D) 105
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: LCM(3,5,7)=105. Answer=105+2=107.

Q87. Find the HCF of (p²q, pq²) where p and q are distinct primes.
     (A) pq  (B) p²q²  (C) p+q  (D) 1
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: p²q=p×(pq); pq²=(pq)×q. HCF=pq.

Q88. For two numbers, HCF=7 and LCM=140. One number is 28. Find the other.
     (A) 35  (B) 42  (C) 49  (D) 21
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: Other=7×140/28=35.

Q89. LCM of 3 numbers = Product/HCF only when:
     (A) Numbers are even  (B) Never  (C) Only for 2 numbers  (D) When all are prime
     ANSWER: C
     EXPLANATION: This identity (LCM×HCF=Product) holds only for pairs of numbers.

Q90. Find LCM(a,b) if a=75 and b=125.
     (A) 375  (B) 750  (C) 300  (D) 625
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: 75=3×5², 125=5³. LCM=3×5³=375.

Q91. The HCF of 2³×3²×5 and 2²×3⁴×7 is:
     (A) 2²×3²  (B) 2³×3⁴×5×7  (C) 2×3  (D) 2²×3
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: HCF = minimum powers: 2²×3²=36.

Q92. Which statement is always true?
     (A) HCF ≥ LCM  (B) HCF ≤ LCM  (C) HCF = LCM  (D) HCF + LCM = product
     ANSWER: B
     EXPLANATION: HCF always divides LCM, so HCF ≤ LCM.

Q93. If HCF(p,q)=p, which is correct?
     (A) p divides q  (B) q divides p  (C) p=q  (D) p=1
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: If HCF=p, then p is a factor of q.

Q94. Find HCF(2310, 630).
     (A) 210  (B) 105  (C) 42  (D) 70
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: 2310=2×3×5×7×11; 630=2×3²×5×7. HCF=2×3×5×7=210.

Q95. Three wires of lengths 64m, 80m, and 96m are to be cut into equal pieces. Find the maximum length of each piece.
     (A) 16m  (B) 8m  (C) 32m  (D) 4m
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: HCF(64,80,96)=16m.

Q96. Find LCM(99, 101).
     (A) 9999  (B) 9901  (C) 10101  (D) 99×101
     ANSWER: D
     EXPLANATION: 99 and 101 are co-prime (99=3²×11, 101 is prime). LCM=99×101=9999. ANSWER: A.

Q97. A school has 96 boys and 120 girls. They are to be arranged in rows with the same number in each row. Find the maximum row size.
     (A) 24  (B) 12  (C) 48  (D) 8
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: HCF(96,120)=24.

Q98. HCF of first 10 prime numbers is:
     (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 30  (D) 2310
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: All primes are distinct, so HCF=1.

Q99. LCM of first 5 prime numbers (2,3,5,7,11) is:
     (A) 2310  (B) 1155  (C) 210  (D) 30
     ANSWER: A
     EXPLANATION: LCM=2×3×5×7×11=2310.

Q100. If N=HCF(a,b)×k, and a=N×m, b=N×n where HCF(m,n)=1, then LCM(a,b)=?
      (A) N×m×n  (B) N×m  (C) N×n  (D) m×n
      ANSWER: A
      EXPLANATION: LCM(a,b)=N×m×n when m and n are coprime.
