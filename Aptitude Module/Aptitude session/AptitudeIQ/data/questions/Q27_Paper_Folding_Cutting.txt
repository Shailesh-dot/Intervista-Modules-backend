================================================================
TOPIC 27: PAPER FOLDING & CUTTING
100 Questions with Options and Answers
================================================================

INSTRUCTIONS: These questions involve folding a square/rectangular
sheet of paper (sometimes making cuts), then unfolding it to
determine the resulting pattern of holes, lines, or shapes.
Visualize each fold carefully before answering.

KEY TERMS:
- Fold line = crease made when paper is folded
- Punch/Hole = made through folded layers; appears symmetrically
  when unfolded
- Cut = straight or curved slice through folded paper

----------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION A: SINGLE FOLD + HOLE PUNCH (Q1–30)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. A square paper is folded in half vertically (left half onto
    right half). One hole is punched in the center of the visible
    (right) half. When unfolded, how many holes appear?
    (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 3  (D) 4
    ANSWER: (B)

Q2. A square paper is folded in half horizontally (top half onto
    bottom half). A hole is punched at the top-right corner of
    the folded paper. When unfolded, holes appear at: ?
    (A) Top-right and bottom-right
    (B) Top-left and top-right
    (C) All four corners
    (D) Center only
    ANSWER: (A)

Q3. A square paper is folded diagonally (bottom-left corner
    meets top-right corner). A hole is punched in the center
    of the folded triangle. When unfolded, how many holes appear?
    (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 3  (D) 4
    ANSWER: (B)

Q4. A square paper is folded vertically, then a hole is punched
    near the fold edge (left side of folded paper). When
    unfolded, the two holes are: ?
    (A) Far apart    (B) Close together near the center
    (C) On same side (D) Diagonally placed
    ANSWER: (B)

Q5. A square paper is folded in half horizontally. A hole is
    punched exactly at the fold line. When unfolded: ?
    (A) 1 hole at the center   (B) 2 holes at fold line positions
    (C) 1 hole at the fold     (D) 4 holes
    ANSWER: (C) [punching on the fold = 1 hole when unfolded]

Q6. A rectangular paper (landscape) is folded left-to-right
    (right half on left half). Two holes are punched vertically
    in the center of the visible half. When unfolded: ?
    (A) 2 holes  (B) 3 holes  (C) 4 holes  (D) 6 holes
    ANSWER: (C)

Q7. A square paper is folded in half vertically. Then folded in
    half again horizontally. One hole is punched. When fully
    unfolded: how many holes?
    (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 4  (D) 8
    ANSWER: (C)

Q8. A square paper is folded: top-right corner to bottom-left

    visible area. When unfolded: ?
    (A) 1 hole  (B) 2 holes  (C) 3 holes  (D) 4 holes
    ANSWER: (B)

Q9. A square paper is folded in half vertically twice (quarter
    width, full height). One hole is punched. When unfolded: ?
    (A) 1  (B) 2  (C) 4  (D) 8
