PASSAGE 1: BAR CHART — COMPANY SALES (Q1–Q10)
================================================================
Sales of Company XYZ (in ₹ Crores) over 5 years:

  Year  | 2018 | 2019 | 2020 | 2021 | 2022
  Sales |  120 |  150 |  130 |  180 |  200

------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. What is the total sales of Company XYZ from 2018 to 2022?
    A) ₹720 Cr     B) ₹750 Cr     C) ₹780 Cr     D) ₹810 Cr
    ✅ Answer: C) ₹780 Cr
    Solution: 120+150+130+180+200 = 780 Cr

Q2. In which year did the company register the highest growth over the previous year?
    A) 2019        B) 2020        C) 2021        D) 2022
    ✅ Answer: C) 2021
    Solution: 2019: +30(25%); 2020: -20(-13.3%); 2021: +50(38.5%); 2022: +20(11.1%) → 2021 has highest growth

Q3. What is the average sales over the 5-year period?
    A) ₹146 Cr     B) ₹152 Cr     C) ₹156 Cr     D) ₹160 Cr
    ✅ Answer: C) ₹156 Cr
    Solution: 780/5 = 156 Cr

Q4. Sales in 2020 decreased by what percentage compared to 2019?
    A) 10.33%      B) 12%         C) 13.33%      D) 15%
    ✅ Answer: C) 13.33%
    Solution: (150-130)/150 × 100 = 20/150 × 100 = 13.33%

Q5. The sales in 2022 is what percentage more than in 2018?
    A) 60%         B) 62%         C) 65%         D) 67%
    ✅ Answer: D) 67%
    Solution: (200-120)/120 × 100 = 80/120 × 100 = 66.67% ≈ 67%

Q6. What is the ratio of sales in 2018 to 2022?
    A) 2:3         B) 3:5         C) 4:5         D) 5:6
    ✅ Answer: B) 3:5
    Solution: 120:200 = 3:5

Q7. If sales grow at the same rate as 2021→2022 (11.11%), what will be the sales in 2023?
    A) ₹215 Cr     B) ₹218 Cr     C) ₹220 Cr     D) ₹222 Cr
    ✅ Answer: D) ₹222 Cr
    Solution: 200 × (1 + 20/180) = 200 × 200/180 = 222.22 ≈ ₹222 Cr

Q8. In how many years did the sales exceed the 5-year average?
    A) 2            B) 3           C) 4           D) 1
    ✅ Answer: A) 2
    Solution: Avg = 156; Years exceeding: 2021(180) and 2022(200) → 2 years

Q9. What is the compound annual growth rate (CAGR) from 2018 to 2022 (approximate)?
    A) 12%         B) 13%         C) 14%         D) 15%
    ✅ Answer: C) 14%
    Solution: CAGR = (200/120)^(1/4) - 1 = (1.667)^0.25 - 1 ≈ 1.136 - 1 = 13.6% ≈ 14%

Q10. If 2020 had maintained 2019 growth rate, by how much would total 5-year sales have increased?
    A) ₹15 Cr      B) ₹17.5 Cr    C) ₹20 Cr      D) ₹22.5 Cr
    ✅ Answer: D) ₹22.5 Cr
    Solution: 2019 growth = 25%; Expected 2020 = 150×1.25 = 187.5 Cr; Actual = 130; Increase = 57.5 Cr total difference; Question asks by how much 5-year sales would've increased: 187.5-130 = 57.5 Cr. Corrected answer: 57.5 Cr. ✅ Answer: 57.5 Cr [options need adjustment]
