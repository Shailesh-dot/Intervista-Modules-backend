NOTE: In "Missing Figure" or "Image Completion" questions, a figure (usually
a square, rectangle, or circle) is divided into sections. One section is
missing (marked with ?). The candidate identifies the correct missing piece
by finding the pattern, rotation, symmetry, or rule that governs the others.

Questions are presented with detailed text descriptions of the figures.
In the actual exam, these would be visual diagrams.

Q1. Study the pattern in the 3×3 matrix below:

   | ○ | ○○ | ○○○ |
   | □ | □□ |  ?  |
   | △ | △△ | △△△ |

   What replaces "?"

   (A)  □    (B)  □□    (C)  □□□    (D)  □□□□

   Answer: (C) □□□
   Explanation: Each row shows an increasing count: 1, 2, 3. Row 2 follows the same pattern.
