================================================================================
         TOPIC 23: WATER IMAGE (REFLECTION)
         100 Standard Questions with Options and Answers
         (Competitive Exam Standard – SSC / Bank / Railway / Defence)
================================================================================

NOTE: A WATER IMAGE is the VERTICAL mirror image — the reflection you see when
      an object is held above a still water surface. The image is flipped
      UPSIDE-DOWN (top becomes bottom, bottom becomes top), while LEFT and RIGHT
      remain UNCHANGED.

      Key Differences:
      • Mirror Image  → Horizontal flip (left ↔ right)
      • Water Image   → Vertical flip   (top ↔ bottom)

Q1. What is the water image of the letter "A"?

   (A)  ∀    (B)  Λ    (C)  V    (D)  A (same)

   Answer: (A) ∀  (inverted A)
   Explanation: Water image flips the letter upside-down. "A" becomes an inverted A (∀).
