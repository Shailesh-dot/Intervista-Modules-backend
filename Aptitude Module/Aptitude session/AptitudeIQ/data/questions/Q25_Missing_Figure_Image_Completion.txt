one figure is missing (marked with ?). Choose the correct option
that logically completes the pattern.

SECTION A: 3×3 MATRIX COMPLETION (Q1–Q40)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. A 3×3 matrix contains shapes that rotate 90° clockwise in
    each row. Row 1: Circle facing right, Circle facing down,
    Circle facing left. Row 2: Square with arrow up, Square with
    arrow right, Square with arrow down. Row 3: Triangle pointing
    up, Triangle pointing right, ?
    (A) Triangle pointing down
    (B) Triangle pointing left
    (C) Triangle pointing up
    (D) Triangle pointing right
    ANSWER: (A)

Q2. In a 3×3 matrix, the number of sides of the shape increases
    by 1 across each row and increases by 1 down each column.
    Row 1: 3,4,5. Row 2: 4,5,6. Row 3: 5,6,?
    (A) 6  (B) 7  (C) 8  (D) 9
    ANSWER: (B)

Q3. A 3×3 matrix has shapes with an inner dot. The dot moves:
    top-left → top-right → bottom-right → bottom-left (clockwise).
    Row 1: dot top-left, dot top-right, dot bottom-right.
    Row 2: dot bottom-left, dot top-left, dot top-right.
    Row 3: dot bottom-right, dot bottom-left, ?
    (A) dot top-left       (B) dot top-right
    (C) dot bottom-right   (D) dot bottom-left
    ANSWER: (A)

Q4. Each row of a 3×3 matrix has circles, and the shading
    pattern follows: white, grey, black across each row.
    Row 3: grey, black, ?
    (A) White  (B) Grey  (C) Black  (D) Striped
    ANSWER: (A)

Q5. In the matrix, each row contains a large, medium, and small
    version of the same shape. Row 3 has: large pentagon, medium
    pentagon, ?
    (A) Large pentagon     (B) Medium pentagon
    (C) Small pentagon     (D) Tiny hexagon
    ANSWER: (C)

Q6. A 3×3 matrix shows stars with varying number of points:
    Row 1: 4,5,6. Row 2: 5,6,7. Row 3: 6,7,?
    (A) 7  (B) 8  (C) 9  (D) 6
    ANSWER: (B)

Q7. Each figure in the matrix is rotated 45° more than the
    previous one across the row. Row 3 starts at 90°, then 135°,
    then ?
    (A) 180°  (B) 160°  (C) 150°  (D) 200°
    ANSWER: (A)

Q8. The number of lines inside each shape equals the row number
    × column number. Row 3, Column 3: ?
    (A) 6  (B) 7  (C) 8  (D) 9
    ANSWER: (D)

Q9. In a 3×3 matrix, each row has a shape with its mirror image
    and then the original again. Row 3: Arrow pointing right,
    Arrow pointing left, ?
    (A) Arrow pointing right  (B) Arrow pointing up
    (C) Arrow pointing down   (D) Arrow pointing left
    ANSWER: (A)

Q10. A 3×3 matrix: shapes change from filled → outline → filled.
     Row 3: outline circle, filled circle, ?
     (A) Outline circle   (B) Filled circle
     (C) Half-filled      (D) Dotted circle
     ANSWER: (A)

Q11. In the grid, the diagonal from top-left to bottom-right
     always has the same shape. Diagonal shapes: square. Row 3,
     Column 3 must be: ?
     (A) Triangle  (B) Square  (C) Circle  (D) Pentagon
     ANSWER: (B)

Q12. Each row contains exactly one each of: a shape with 0, 1,
     and 2 dots inside. Row 3: shape with 2 dots, shape with 0
     dots, ?
     (A) Shape with 1 dot   (B) Shape with 2 dots
     (C) Shape with 3 dots  (D) Shape with 0 dots
     ANSWER: (A)

Q13. Shapes in the matrix flip horizontally each column.
     Column 3 always shows the original orientation.
     Row 3, Column 2: flipped arrow. Row 3, Column 3: ?
     (A) Flipped arrow     (B) Original arrow
     (C) Rotated arrow     (D) Upward arrow
     ANSWER: (B)

Q14. Sum of sides of shapes in each row = 12.
     Row 3: hexagon (6) + triangle (3) + ?
     (A) 2  (B) 3  (C) 4  (D) 5
     ANSWER: (B) [triangle = 3 sides]

Q15. In a 3×3 matrix, columns 1,2,3 always contain shapes in
     order: square, circle, triangle. Row 3, Column 3: ?
     (A) Square  (B) Circle  (C) Triangle  (D) Pentagon
     ANSWER: (C)

Q16. Each shape is shaded: Row 1 = 25%, Row 2 = 50%, Row 3 = 75%.
     Row 3, Column 2 has 75% shading. Row 3, Column 3: ?
     (A) 25%  (B) 50%  (C) 75%  (D) 100%
     ANSWER: (C)

Q17. In the matrix, shapes increase in size: small → medium →
     large going down each column. Column 3: small heart, medium
     heart, ?
     (A) Small heart   (B) Medium heart
     (C) Large heart   (D) Extra-large heart
     ANSWER: (C)

Q18. A 3×3 matrix has arrows. Each row shows a rotation of
     90°. Row 3 starts pointing south, then west, then ?
     (A) North  (B) East  (C) South  (D) West
     ANSWER: (A)

Q19. Each row's shapes contain 1, 2, 3 dots respectively.
     Row 3: 3 dots, 1 dot, ?
     (A) 2 dots  (B) 3 dots  (C) 1 dot  (D) 4 dots
     ANSWER: (A)

Q20. A 3×3 matrix shows patterns of lines inside rectangles:
     horizontal, vertical, diagonal. Row 3: diagonal, horizontal, ?
     (A) Diagonal    (B) Horizontal

Q21. In the matrix, shapes are: filled circle, ring, filled

     (A) Ring           (B) Filled circle

Q22. Size of shape (in units) follows: Col1=3, Col2=2, Col3=1

     (A) Largest   (B) Medium   (C) Smallest   (D) Equal

Q23. In a 3×3 matrix of clocks, each clock advances by 2 hours

Q24. Each row has a shape divided into: 2, 4, 8 equal parts.
     Row 3: 8 parts, 2 parts, ?
     (A) 4 parts  (B) 6 parts  (C) 8 parts  (D) 10 parts
     ANSWER: (A)

Q25. A 3×3 matrix uses three shapes (circle, square, pentagon)

     Row 3, Col 3 is missing. Row 3 has circle, pentagon, ?
     (A) Circle  (B) Square  (C) Pentagon  (D) Triangle
     ANSWER: (B)

Q26. Shapes in the matrix alternate between outlined and filled
     along the anti-diagonal. Anti-diagonal bottom item: ?
     If pattern is filled → outline → filled: ?
     (A) Outlined  (B) Filled  (C) Striped  (D) Dotted
     ANSWER: (A)

Q27. In a 3×3 matrix, each row has: shape with 4 sides, 5 sides,
     6 sides. Row 3: pentagon (5), hexagon (6), ?
     (A) Triangle (3)   (B) Square (4)
     (C) Pentagon (5)   (D) Heptagon (7)
     ANSWER: (B)

Q28. The pattern across each row: shape, its reflection, original.
     Row 3: F-shape, mirrored F, ?
     (A) Mirrored F   (B) F-shape
     (C) Rotated F    (D) Upside-down F
     ANSWER: (B)

Q29. Each cell shading increases by 1 level (1–9 levels total,
     reading left-to-right, top-to-bottom). Cell 9 (Row 3,
     Col 3): ?
     (A) Lightest  (B) Medium  (C) Darkest  (D) White
     ANSWER: (C)

Q30. A 3×3 matrix of pentagons: each rotated 72° more than the
     previous. Row 3, Col 3: Row 3 starts at 144°, Col2 = 216°,
     Col 3 = ?
     (A) 270°  (B) 288°  (C) 300°  (D) 252°

Q31. In each row, number of black squares inside the shape =
     row number. Row 3: shapes have 3 black squares. Row 3,
     Col 3 shows: 1 black square, 2 black squares, ?
     (A) 2  (B) 3  (C) 4  (D) 5
     ANSWER: (B)

Q32. The matrix has shapes connected with 0, 1, 2 lines to their
     borders respectively across each row. Row 3: 2 lines, 0 lines, ?
     (A) 1 line  (B) 2 lines  (C) 0 lines  (D) 3 lines
     ANSWER: (A)

Q33. Each shape contains an inner shape that is one size smaller.
     Row 3, Col 3 has a medium triangle inside it. The outer
     shape must be: ?
     (A) Small triangle   (B) Medium triangle
     (C) Large triangle   (D) Square
     ANSWER: (C)

Q34. In a 3×3 matrix, shape complexity increases diagonally.
     The bottom-right is always the most complex. Row 3, Col 3: ?
     (A) Simple circle     (B) Complex hexagon with inner shapes

Q35. Each row has the same shape but a different number of

     (A) 0 lines  (B) 1 line  (C) 2 lines  (D) 3 lines

Q36. A 3×3 matrix contains letters rotating 90° clockwise:
     Row 1: P, d, q. Row 2: b, P, d. Row 3: q, b, ?

Q37. The shapes in the matrix have striped patterns:

Q38. A 3×3 matrix: each column sums to 15 (numeric values).
     Col 3: 4, 6, ?

Q39. In the matrix, arrows point in these directions per row
     (in degrees): Row 1: 0°, 90°, 180°. Row 2: 90°, 180°, 270°.

Q40. Each row's shapes have sides = 3, 4, 5 but the ORDER

SECTION C: ANALOGY-BASED IMAGE COMPLETION (Q71–100)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q71. Circle : Sphere :: Square : ?
     (A) Rectangle  (B) Cube  (C) Cylinder  (D) Cone
     ANSWER: (B)

Q72. A figure with 3 lines of symmetry : equilateral triangle ::
     A figure with 4 lines of symmetry : ?
     (A) Circle    (B) Pentagon
     (C) Square    (D) Hexagon
     ANSWER: (C)

Q73. If a shape inside a larger shape is the same but half the
     size, this is called: ?
     (A) Scaling    (B) Reflection
     (C) Translation (D) Rotation
     ANSWER: (A)

Q74. Arrow pointing right in a box → Arrow pointing left out of
     the box :: Arrow pointing up in a box → ?
     (A) Arrow pointing up out of box
     (B) Arrow pointing down out of box
     (C) Arrow pointing left out of box
     (D) Arrow pointing right out of box
     ANSWER: (B)

Q75. Solid triangle → hollow triangle :: Solid rectangle → ?
     (A) Hollow rectangle  (B) Solid square
     (C) Hollow circle     (D) Solid pentagon
     ANSWER: (A)

Q76. Black circle on white background → White circle on black
     background. The transformation is: ?
     (A) Rotation    (B) Inversion/Negative
     (C) Reflection  (D) Translation
     ANSWER: (B)

Q77. 3-sided shape → △ :: 4-sided shape → □ :: 5-sided shape → ?
     (A) ○  (B) ⬠  (C) ⬡  (D) ⬟
     ANSWER: (B)

Q78. Figure A has 4 internal divisions. Figure B has the same
     shape with 8 internal divisions. Figure C has 16 internal
     divisions. Figure D: ?
     (A) 20 divisions  (B) 24 divisions
     (C) 32 divisions  (D) 28 divisions
     ANSWER: (C)

Q79. Shape X rotated 90° clockwise = Shape Y. Shape Y rotated
     90° clockwise = Shape Z. Shape Z rotated 90° clockwise = ?
     (A) Shape X    (B) Shape Y
     (C) Shape Z    (D) New shape
     ANSWER: (A)

Q80. Image shows two overlapping circles (Venn diagram style).
     The shaded region is the intersection. If circles don't
     overlap, shaded region is: ?
     (A) Both circles      (B) Empty/None
     (C) One circle only   (D) Outer region
     ANSWER: (B)

Q81. If figure A is reflected over a vertical axis to get
     figure B, then figure B reflected over the same axis
     gives: ?
     (A) Figure A    (B) Figure B
     (C) New figure  (D) Rotated figure
     ANSWER: (A)

Q82. A square's diagonal = d. A square with double the area has
     diagonal = ?
     (A) 2d     (B) d√2   (C) d/2   (D) 4d
     ANSWER: (B)

Q83. Pattern: square → circle appears inside square → triangle
     appears inside circle → ?
     (A) Pentagon inside triangle
     (B) Square inside triangle
     (C) Circle inside triangle
     (D) Diamond inside triangle
     ANSWER: (B)

Q84. Figure: a right-pointing arrow is flipped upside down →

     J-shape flipped upside down becomes: ?
     (A) J-shape     (B) L-shape
     (C) Γ-shape     (D) ∩-shape

     rectangle. A = right triangle, B = right triangle, C = ?
     (A) Square      (B) Rectangle
     (C) Right triangle  (D) Circle
