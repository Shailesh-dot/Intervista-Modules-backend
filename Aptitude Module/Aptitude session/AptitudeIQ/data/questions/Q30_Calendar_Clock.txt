100 Questions with Options and Answers

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
■ CLOCK:
  - Minute hand speed: 6°/min (360°/60 min)
  - Hour hand speed: 0.5°/min (360°/720 min)
  - Relative speed of minute hand over hour hand: 5.5°/min
  - Angle between hands at H:MM = |30H - 5.5M|°
  - Hands coincide: every 65 5/11 minutes
  - Hands are at right angles: 22 times in 12 hours
  - Hands are opposite (180°): 11 times in 12 hours

■ CALENDAR:
  - Leap year: divisible by 4; century years divisible by 400
  - Odd days: Jan=3, Feb=0(non-leap)/1(leap), Mar=3, Apr=2,
    May=3, Jun=2, Jul=3, Aug=3, Sep=2, Oct=3, Nov=2, Dec=3
  - Days in week: Mon=1, Tue=2, Wed=3, Thu=4, Fri=5, Sat=6, Sun=0

Q1. At 3:00, what is the angle between the hour and minute hands?
    (A) 60°   (B) 75°   (C) 90°   (D) 120°
    ANSWER: (C) [30×3 = 90°]

Q2. At 6:00, the angle between the hour and minute hands is: ?
    (A) 90°   (B) 120°   (C) 180°   (D) 270°
    ANSWER: (C)

Q3. At 12:00 (noon), the angle between the hands is: ?
    (A) 0°   (B) 90°   (C) 180°   (D) 360°
    ANSWER: (A)

Q4. What is the angle between the hands of a clock at 4:20?
    Formula: |30×4 - 5.5×20| = |120 - 110| = 10°
    (A) 5°   (B) 10°   (C) 15°   (D) 20°
    ANSWER: (B)

Q5. At 9:30, the angle between hour and minute hands is: ?
    |30×9 - 5.5×30| = |270 - 165| = 105°
    (A) 90°   (B) 95°   (C) 100°   (D) 105°
    ANSWER: (D)

Q6. At what time between 3 and 4 o'clock are the hands coincident?
    Formula: 3×60/11 + 3×60 = ? → At 3 o'clock, gap = 15 min spaces.
    Time = 15 × 12/11 = 180/11 = 16 4/11 min past 3.
    (A) 3:15   (B) 3:16 4/11   (C) 3:20   (D) 3:14
    ANSWER: (B)

Q7. How many times do the minute and hour hands coincide in 24 hours?
    (A) 22   (B) 24   (C) 44   (D) 48
    ANSWER: (C) [22 times in 12 hours × 2 = 44]

Q8. At 7:35, the angle between the clock hands is: ?
    |30×7 - 5.5×35| = |210 - 192.5| = 17.5°
    (A) 10°   (B) 12.5°   (C) 17.5°   (D) 22.5°
    ANSWER: (C)

Q9. At what time between 5 and 6 is the minute hand exactly
    3 minutes behind the hour hand (in minute spaces)?
    At 5:00, hour hand is at 25 min mark.
    Minute hand needs to be at 22 min mark → 22 minutes past 5.
    Check: hour hand at 22 min past 5 = 25 + 22/12 = 25 + 1.83 = 26.83 min mark.
    Not exactly right. Standard approach:
    (A) 5:22   (B) 5:24   (C) 5:20   (D) 5:18
    ANSWER: (B) [~5:24 when 3-minute gap exists; exact = complex]

Q10. A clock shows 8:20. What time does its mirror image show?
     Mirror of 8:20 = 12:00 - 8:20 = 3:40
     (A) 3:40   (B) 4:40   (C) 3:20   (D) 4:20
     ANSWER: (A)

Q11. A clock gains 5 minutes every hour. If set correctly at
     12:00 noon, what time does it show when the actual time is
     6:00 PM? (6 hours later)
     (A) 6:00   (B) 6:30   (C) 6:25   (D) 6:35
     ANSWER: (B) [5 min × 6 hours = 30 min fast; shows 6:30]

Q12. How many times in a day does a clock's minute hand pass
     the 12 on the clock face?
     (A) 12   (B) 24   (C) 36   (D) 48
     ANSWER: (B)

Q13. The minute hand of a clock is 7 cm long. How far does it
     travel in 30 minutes?
     (A) 7π cm   (B) 14π cm   (C) 7π/2 cm   (D) 21π cm
     ANSWER: (A) [half circumference = πr = 7π]

Q14. At 2:45, the minute hand is pointing to: ?
     (A) 2   (B) 8   (C) 9   (D) 3
     ANSWER: (C) [45 min = 9 on clock face]

Q15. At 10:10, the hour hand is between which numbers?
     (A) 10 and 11   (B) 9 and 10   (C) 10 and 12   (D) 11 and 12
     ANSWER: (A) [at 10:10, hour hand moved 10/60 × 30° = 5° past 10]

Q16. How many right angles does the minute hand make in 1 hour?
     (A) 2   (B) 3   (C) 4   (D) 6
     ANSWER: (C) [every 15 min = right angle → 4 times per hour approximately]
     [Actually right angles relative to hour hand: ~22 in 12 hrs; but relative to a fixed direction: 4 per hour]
     ANSWER: (C)

Q17. A watch is 10 minutes slow. If the watch shows 4:30, the

     (A) 4:20   (B) 4:30   (C) 4:40   (D) 5:00

Q18. Between 7 and 8 o'clock, when are the hands of a clock

     For 90°: hands 15 min apart.
     35 - 15 = 20 min to close → time = 20 × 12/11 = 240/11 min past 7
     = 21 9/11 min past 7.
     (A) 7:21 9/11   (B) 7:25   (C) 7:22   (D) 7:20

Q19. In a 12-hour period, how many times are the clock hands

Q20. If a clock is placed face-down and you look at the back,

Q23. At what time between 4 and 5 is the minute hand exactly
     opposite to the hour hand (180° apart)?
     At 4:00, gap = 20 min. Need gap = 30 min.
     Time = (30-20) × 12/11 = 120/11 = 10 10/11 min past 4
     = 4:10 10/11
     (A) 4:45   (B) 4:10 10/11   (C) 4:30   (D) 4:54 6/11
     ANSWER: (D) [54 6/11 min: recalculate: at 4, gap=20. For 180°: need 30 min gap.
                   Rate = 5.5 min/min. Time to add 10 min of space = 10/5.5 × 60... 
                   Actually: gap needed = 30-20=10 spaces to gain. At 5.5 spaces/min: 
                   time = 10/5.5 = 20/11 min... No: 
                   Standard: hands opposite when minute hand is 30 min ahead in spaces.
                   At 4:00, minute=0, hour=20. For opposite: minute - hour = 30 (mod 60).
                   M - 5.5t/60×60... use formula: t = (H×30 ∓ 180)/5.5
                   At 4: t = (4×30-180)/5.5 = (120-180)/5.5 = negative → use +180
                   t = (120+180)/5.5 = 300/5.5 = 54 6/11]
     ANSWER: (D)

Q28. At 3:15, the angle between the hands is: ?
     |30×3 - 5.5×15| = |90 - 82.5| = 7.5°

     (A) 6:00   (B) 5:54   (C) 6:10   (D) 5:58

Q30. At what time between 9 and 10 are the hands of a clock

Q31. A digital clock shows 12:48. How many hours until it shows

Q32. Between 1 and 2 o'clock, at what time are the hands at
     right angles for the first time?
     At 1:00, gap = 5 min. For 90° (15 min spaces):
     Need to gain 10 min spaces. Time = 10/5.5 × 60... 
     = 10 × 12/11 = 120/11 = 10 10/11 min past 1 = 1:10 10/11.
     (A) 1:10 10/11   (B) 1:15   (C) 1:12   (D) 1:20
     ANSWER: (A)

Q33. A clock face shows 10:10. What is the REFLEX angle
     (larger angle) between the hands?
     Regular angle = |300 - 55| = 245°? Wait: |30×10 - 5.5×10| = |300-55| = 245°.
     Reflex of 245° = 360-245 = 115°. So the SMALLER angle = 115°, larger = 245°.
     (A) 115°   (B) 245°   (C) 180°   (D) 270°
     ANSWER: (B) [245° is the reflex angle]

Q40. Between 2 and 3 o'clock, at what time are the hands of a

     Gain 20 more min. Time = 20 × 12/11 = 240/11 = 21 9/11 min past 2.
     (A) 2:21 9/11   (B) 2:30   (C) 2:43 7/11   (D) 2:50

SECTION B: CALENDAR PROBLEMS (Q51–100)

Q51. Which of the following years is a leap year?
     (A) 1900   (B) 2000   (C) 2100   (D) 1800
     ANSWER: (B)

Q52. How many days in a leap year?
     (A) 355   (B) 365   (C) 366   (D) 367
     ANSWER: (C)

Q53. January 1, 2000 was a Saturday. What day was January 1, 2001?
     2000 is a leap year, so 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 days.
     Saturday + 2 = Monday.
     (A) Sunday   (B) Monday   (C) Tuesday   (D) Wednesday
     ANSWER: (B)

Q54. If March 1 is a Thursday, what day is April 1 of the same year?
     March has 31 days. 31 = 4 weeks + 3 days.
     Thursday + 3 = Sunday.
     (A) Friday   (B) Saturday   (C) Sunday   (D) Monday
     ANSWER: (C)

Q55. How many odd days in 100 years?
     (A) 1   (B) 2   (C) 5   (D) 6
     ANSWER: (C) [76 ordinary years + 24 leap years = 76 + 24×2 = 76+48 = 124 odd days; 124 mod 7 = 5]

Q56. If today is Wednesday, what day is 100 days from today?
     100 = 14 × 7 + 2. Wednesday + 2 = Friday.
     (A) Thursday   (B) Friday   (C) Saturday   (D) Sunday
     ANSWER: (B)

Q57. February 29 occurs in:
     (A) Every 4 years only
     (B) Every century year
     (C) Leap years (every 4 years except century years not
         divisible by 400)
     (D) Every other year
     ANSWER: (C)

Q58. How many Sundays in a normal (non-leap) year?
     365 days = 52 weeks + 1 day. If that extra day is Sunday: 53. Otherwise: 52.
     (A) 51   (B) 52   (C) 52 or 53   (D) 53
     ANSWER: (C)

Q59. What day of the week was 1 January 1901?
     1 Jan 1900 = Monday (known reference). 1900 is NOT a leap year.
     365 days = 52 weeks + 1 day. So 1 Jan 1901 = Monday + 1 = Tuesday.
     (A) Monday   (B) Tuesday   (C) Wednesday   (D) Thursday
     ANSWER: (B)

Q60. If a year starts on Friday and is a leap year, how many
     Fridays are there in that year?
     366 days = 52 × 7 + 2. The two extra days are Friday and Saturday.
     So there are 53 Fridays.
     (A) 52   (B) 53   (C) 54   (D) 51
     ANSWER: (B)

Q61. A person was born on 29 February 1996. On which date did
     they celebrate their 4th actual birthday?
     Next Feb 29 after 1996: 2000 (leap year).
     (A) Feb 28, 2000   (B) Feb 29, 2000   (C) Mar 1, 2000   (D) Feb 29, 1997
     ANSWER: (B)

Q62. What is the maximum number of times a particular date
     (like the 13th) can fall on a Friday in a year?
     (A) 1   (B) 2   (C) 3   (D) 4
     ANSWER: (C) [Friday the 13th occurs 1, 2, or 3 times per year]

Q63. How many odd days in 400 years?
     (A) 0   (B) 1   (C) 2   (D) 5
     ANSWER: (A) [The Gregorian calendar repeats every 400 years = 0 odd days net]

Q64. Today is the 15th of a month. 45 days later will be the: ?
     15 + 45 = 60. Depends on month. In general: 15+45=60th day from start of month.
     If in January: Jan has 31 days; 60-31=29; 31+29=60 → Feb 29 or Mar 1.
     (A) Depends on the month and year
     (B) Always the 30th of the next month
     (C) Always the 29th of next month
     (D) Always the 1st of the month after next
     ANSWER: (A)

Q65. If the first day of a century (January 1) is Tuesday,
     what day is January 2 of the same year?
     (A) Monday   (B) Tuesday   (C) Wednesday   (D) Thursday
     ANSWER: (C)

Q66. A meeting is scheduled every 10 days starting on a Monday.
     The 8th meeting falls on: ?
     Day = Monday + (8-1) × 10 = Monday + 70 days.
     70 = 10 × 7 = exactly 10 weeks. Same day = Monday.
     (A) Monday   (B) Tuesday   (C) Wednesday   (D) Thursday
     ANSWER: (A)

Q67. Which months have 30 days?
     (A) April, June, September, November
     (B) April, June, September, October
     (C) April, July, September, November
     (D) February, April, June, September
     ANSWER: (A)

Q68. How many days from January 15 to March 15 (non-leap year)?
     Jan: 31-15=16 days remaining; Feb=28; Mar 1-15=15.
     Total = 16+28+15 = 59 days.
     (A) 58   (B) 59   (C) 60   (D) 61
     ANSWER: (B)

Q69. A man was born on the 5th day of the month. His brother was
     born exactly 2 years 3 months and 5 days later. On what
     date was his brother born?
     5 + 5 = 10th day; month + 3; year + 2 (plus 1 if months carry over).
     (A) 10th, 3 months later   (B) 5th, 3 months later
     (C) 10th, same month       (D) Exact date depends on months
     ANSWER: (A)

Q70. In a non-leap year, the 100th day falls in: ?
     Jan=31, Feb=28, Mar=31. 31+28=59. 100-59=41. 41-31(Mar)=10. → April 10.
     (A) March 31   (B) April 9   (C) April 10   (D) April 11
     ANSWER: (C)

Q71. If January 1 of a particular year is a Sunday, what day
     is July 4 of the same (non-leap) year?
     Days from Jan 1 to July 4:
     Jan=31, Feb=28, Mar=31, Apr=30, May=31, Jun=30, Jul 1-4=4.
     Total from Jan 2 to Jul 4 = 31+28+31+30+31+30+3 = 184 days.
     Total from Jan 1: 183 days have passed after Jan 1. So it's the 184th day.
     184 mod 7 = 184 - 26×7 = 184-182 = 2. Sunday + 2 = Tuesday? Let me recount.
     Jan 1 = day 1 (Sunday). Jul 4 = day 185.
     (185-1) = 184 days after Sunday. 184/7 = 26 weeks + 2. Sunday+2 = Tuesday.
     (A) Monday   (B) Tuesday   (C) Wednesday   (D) Thursday
     ANSWER: (B)

Q72. How many days are in February 2096?
     2096 ÷ 4 = 524 (divisible). Is it a century year? No (not 2100).
     So it's a leap year. February has 29 days.
     (A) 28   (B) 29   (C) 30   (D) 31
     ANSWER: (B)

Q73. February 2100 has how many days?
     2100 ÷ 100 = 21 (century year). 2100 ÷ 400 = 5.25 (NOT divisible). Not a leap year.
     (A) 28   (B) 29   (C) 30   (D) 27
     ANSWER: (A)

Q74. A man remembers an appointment is between the 10th and 15th
     of a month, not on Thursday or Sunday. The date is either 11

Q75. A project starts on October 1 and lasts exactly 75 days.

Q76. Ravi was born on 16th August 1995. His friend Priya was born

     (A) 1992   (B) 1993   (C) 1994   (D) 1998

Q77. If a week starts on Wednesday, the 15th day of that period is: ?

Q78. How many years in a century have 366 days?
     Normal: 25 years divisible by 4; but century year itself:

     If divisible by 400 (like 2000): 25 leap years.
     (A) 24 or 25   (B) 25 always   (C) 24 always   (D) 26

Q79. If today is Tuesday, 3rd, what day is the 17th of the same month?
     17-3=14 days later. 14 mod 7 = 0. Same day.
     (A) Tuesday   (B) Wednesday   (C) Saturday   (D) Monday
     ANSWER: (A)

Q80. A lunar month is approximately 29.5 days. How many lunar
     months are there in a solar year (365 days)?
     365 / 29.5 ≈ 12.37.
     (A) 12   (B) 12.37   (C) 13   (D) 11.5
     ANSWER: (B) [approximately 12.37; in practice ~12 to 13]

Q81. What day of the week is December 25, if January 1 of that
     year (non-leap) is a Friday?
     Jan 1 = Friday. Dec 25 = day 359.
     (359-1) = 358 days after Friday. 358 mod 7 = 358-51×7 = 358-357 = 1.
     Friday + 1 = Saturday.
     (A) Friday   (B) Saturday   (C) Sunday   (D) Monday
     ANSWER: (B)

Q82. A person's birthday is on February 29. In how many years
     will they celebrate on Feb 29 for the 5th time?
     First birthday: year 0. 5th = 4 more leap years after birth year.
     Usually: birth year (1st), +4, +8, +12, +16 (5th).
     But: some 4-year intervals skip (e.g., 1896→1904 skips 1900).
     Approximately: 16 years (could be more if century year is skipped).
     (A) 16 years   (B) 17 years   (C) 20 years   (D) 12 years
     ANSWER: (A)

Q84. A government year budget runs from April 1 to March 31.
     If April 1 is a Monday, what day is March 31 (end of year)?

     Last day (March 31) = April 1 + 364 days. 364 = 52×7. Same day = Monday.

Q92. What is the day of the week for October 2, 2002?
