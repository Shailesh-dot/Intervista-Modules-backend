CONCEPTS & RULES:
─────────────────
1. SHADOW is always cast OPPOSITE to the direction of light.
2. Shadow falls on the SAME SIDE as the direction AWAY from the light source.
3. Morning Sun (East): Shadow falls towards WEST
4. Evening Sun (West): Shadow falls towards EAST
5. Noon Sun (overhead/South in Northern Hemisphere): Very short shadow (South)
6. North Sun (rare, used in problems): Shadow falls towards SOUTH

KEY RULE TABLE:
   Light from EAST  → Shadow points WEST
   Light from WEST  → Shadow points EAST
   Light from NORTH → Shadow points SOUTH
   Light from SOUTH → Shadow points NORTH
   Light from NE    → Shadow points SW
   Light from NW    → Shadow points SE
   Light from SE    → Shadow points NW
   Light from SW    → Shadow points NE
   Light from TOP   → Shadow falls directly BELOW (no directional shadow)

STANDARD EXAM APPROACH:
  "A person stands facing [direction]. The sun is in the [direction] at [time].
   Determine the direction of the shadow."

Q1. In the morning, the sun rises in the East. A pole is standing vertically.
   In which direction will its shadow fall?

   (A)  East    (B)  West    (C)  North    (D)  South

   Answer: (B) West
   Explanation: Morning sun is in the East → shadow falls opposite = West.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q2. In the evening, the sun is in the West. A tree is standing upright.
   In which direction does its shadow fall?

   (A)  North    (B)  West    (C)  East    (D)  South

   Answer: (C) East
   Explanation: Sun in West → shadow falls East.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q3. The sun is exactly in the South at noon (Northern Hemisphere).
   A vertical stick is placed in the ground. Where does its shadow fall?

   (A)  East    (B)  West    (C)  South    (D)  North

   Answer: (D) North
   Explanation: Sun in the South → shadow falls towards North.

   (A)  6 AM    (B)  9 AM    (C)  12 Noon    (D)  3 PM

   Answer: (C) 12 Noon
   Explanation: At noon, the sun is at its highest point, so the shadow is shortest.

Q62. At what time of day is a person's shadow the LONGEST?

   (A)  12 Noon    (B)  Sunrise or Sunset    (C)  3 PM    (D)  9 AM

   Answer: (B) Sunrise or Sunset
   Explanation: When the sun is at the horizon (low angle), shadows are longest.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q63. A 6-foot tall man casts a shadow of 10 feet at 3:00 PM.
   A nearby pole casts a shadow of 25 feet at the same time.
   What is the height of the pole?

   (A)  10 feet    (B)  12 feet    (C)  15 feet    (D)  20 feet

   Answer: (C) 15 feet
   Explanation: Using proportion: 6/10 = height/25 → height = (6 × 25)/10 = 15 feet.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q64. A tree casts a shadow of 20 m at the same time a 5 m pole casts a shadow of 4 m.
   What is the height of the tree?

   (A)  20 m    (B)  25 m    (C)  16 m    (D)  18 m

   Answer: (B) 25 m
   Explanation: 5/4 = h/20 → h = (5 × 20)/4 = 25 m.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

   Answer: (B) 10 m
   Explanation: 30/18 = h/6 → h = (30 × 6)/18 = 10 m.

   (A)  15m    (B)  15√3 m    (C)  15/√3 m    (D)  30 m

   Answer: (C) 15/√3 m ≈ 8.66 m
   Explanation: tan(60°) = 15/shadow → shadow = 15/√3.

Q71. A vertical pole and its shadow make an angle of 45°.
   If the shadow is 12 m, what is the height of the pole?

   (A)  6 m    (B)  12 m    (C)  12√2 m    (D)  24 m

   Answer: (B) 12 m
   Explanation: At 45°, shadow length equals height → 12 m.

Q72. A person observes that the shadow length equals twice their height.
   What is the approximate angle of the sun above the horizon?

   (A)  26.6°    (B)  45°    (C)  60°    (D)  30°

   Answer: (A) 26.6°
   Explanation: tan(θ) = 1/2 = 0.5 → θ ≈ 26.6°.

Q73. During the summer solstice, at solar noon at the Tropic of Cancer (23.5°N),

   (C)  To the South    (D)  To the East

   Answer: (B) Directly beneath it (no shadow — sun directly overhead)
   Explanation: At summer solstice noon on the Tropic of Cancer, the sun is directly overhead.

Q74. On the equator at equinox at solar noon, the shadow of a vertical stick falls:

   (A)  North    (B)  South    (C)  Directly below (no shadow)    (D)  East

   Answer: (C) Directly below (no shadow)
   Explanation: At equinox noon on the equator, the sun is directly overhead.

Q75. A shadow of length 8 m is cast by a pole of height 8 m.

   (A)  Top-right    (B)  Bottom-left    (C)  Top-left    (D)  Bottom-right

   Explanation: Shadow is opposite to the light source.

Q79. In a figure, a cube has its shadow falling toward the bottom-right.
   The light source is located:

   (A)  Bottom-right    (B)  Top-left    (C)  Top-right    (D)  Bottom-left

   Explanation: Shadow at bottom-right → light from top-left.

Q80. A person casts a shadow to the North. The observer faces South.
   For the observer, the shadow falls:

   (A)  To their front    (B)  To their back    (C)  To their left    (D)  To their right

   Answer: (B) To their back (North is behind a South-facing observer)
   Explanation: Observer faces South; North is behind them → shadow falls behind observer.

Q81. It is early morning. A soldier stands at attention facing the rising sun.
   His shadow falls:

   (A)  In front of him    (B)  Behind him    (C)  To his left    (D)  To his right

   Answer: (B) Behind him
   Explanation: Sun is in the East (in front as he faces East) → shadow falls West = behind.

Q82. A man walks East in the morning. As the day progresses and the sun moves higher,
   his shadow:

   (A)  Grows longer
   (B)  Stays the same length
   (C)  Grows shorter
   (D)  Disappears

   Answer: (C) Grows shorter
   Explanation: As the sun rises higher, shadows shorten.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q83. On a completely overcast (cloudy) day, a vertical pole's shadow:

   (A)  Falls to the West    (B)  Falls to the North
   (C)  Is diffuse (no clear shadow direction)    (D)  Falls longer than sunny day

   Answer: (C) Is diffuse (no clear shadow direction)
   Explanation: Clouds scatter light in all directions, resulting in no sharp shadow.

Q84. At 3:00 PM in the afternoon (sun in Southwest), a man stands facing East.
   His shadow falls to his:

   (A)  Front    (B)  Back    (C)  Left    (D)  Right

   Answer: (B) Back
   Note: Sun SW → shadow NE. Man faces East. NE is to the front-left of an East-facing person.
   Correction: shadow NE from East-facing man = shadow is ahead-left (slightly front-left, but NE is front-left when facing East).
   Practical exam answer: (A) Front-left or (C) Left depending on exam convention.
   Standard Answer: (A) slightly in front (NE while facing East)

Q85. A person is standing with her face to the sun in the morning.
   Her shadow will fall:

   (A)  In front of her    (B)  Behind her    (C)  To her left    (D)  To her right

   (B)  Equal to his height

Q88. If the sun is in the Southwest and a person is facing Northwest,

   (A)  To their left    (B)  To their right    (C)  In front    (D)  Behind

   Shadow NE = to the RIGHT. Answer: (B) To their right.
